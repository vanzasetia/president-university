Welcome to President University

Where tomorrow's leaders come together

President University is devoted to excellence in teaching, learning, and research, and to developing leaders in many disciplines who make a difference globally.

The University, which is based in Jababeka industrial park, Cikarang, is the largest industrial estate in SEA and is home to 1,700 multinational companies from 30 countries [USA, Canada, UK, Germany, France, the Netherlands, Korea, Japan, China, Taiwan, Malaysia, Singapore, Australia], has an enrollment of over 2,000 degree candidates every year, including undergraduate, graduate, and professional students.

Admission and Application requirements

All freshman applicants—both international and Indonesian candidates—must complete the Common Application along with the required documents. You will need to submit:

  1. a copy of your KTP or student ID card for international students and the certified copy of pages of your passport stating your full name, date and place of birth, and expiration date of the passport.
  2. a certified copy of your original academic diploma in English [for international students]
  3. a certified copy of your transcript (list of grades/marks) in the original language,
  4. a certified translation of your final transcript(list of grades/marks) in English,

Explanation of "certified copies"

Certified copies are either copies that bear the original logo or stamp of [1] the institute [the school] which issued the original document [2] an Indonesian Embassy or consulate, [3] translations that bear the stamp of the sworn translator who translated the original document.

Please refer to our www.president.ac.id for academic and admission schedules.

Required English standardized tests

A strong knowledge of English is essential for successful study at President University, including the ability to understand and express thoughts quickly and clearly. We require standardized English test scores at a minimum of 400 from TOEIC [Test of English for International Communication] in the freshman first year.

Although you are not required to take the Test of English as a Foreign Language (TOEFL) or other proficiency exams, you may submit your scores if you have done so.
